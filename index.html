A Day in Asset Operations — What Actually Happens Behind the Scenes

By Aravind · Asset Operations Intern · Chennai

Most people think fintech is about apps, UI, and transactions.

But behind every transaction, there’s a layer of operations that keeps everything running smoothly.

Working in asset operations, I’ve realized the real work is often invisible—but critical.

1. It starts with understanding the deal

Every day doesn’t start with execution—it starts with context. Understanding the deal structure, stakeholders involved, and how funds are supposed to flow is key before doing anything.

2. Financial model creation

Before funds move, numbers need to make sense. Creating and validating financial models helps in understanding returns, timelines, and structuring deals properly.

3. Agreement initiation

A major part of the process is documentation. This includes initiating and coordinating agreements like:

  • Discounting Agreements
  • Indemnity Bonds
  • Guarantee Agreements
  • Term Sheets
Each document plays a role in ensuring clarity, compliance, and risk coverage.

4. VBA creation and KYC

Setting up Virtual Bank Accounts (VBAs) is critical for tracking fund flows. Along with that, KYC processes ensure that all parties involved are verified and compliant.

5. Vendor and system setup

Before transactions can happen, backend systems need to be ready. This includes:

  • Vendor ID creation
  • Asset card creation for tracking deals
These systems ensure everything is recorded and traceable.

6. Fund flow coordination

Once everything is in place, execution begins. Coordinating with finance teams on fund allocation, tracking balances, and ensuring funds are utilized correctly becomes the focus.

7. TDS and compliance coordination

Transactions are not just about movement of money—they also involve compliance. Coordinating TDS deductions and ensuring regulatory requirements are met is part of the workflow.

8. Visibility and communication

Stakeholders constantly need updates—balances, statements, and transaction statuses. Providing clear visibility builds trust across all parties involved.

What I’ve learned

Asset operations is not just about handling funds. It sits at the intersection of finance, compliance, and coordination.

It has taught me how important attention to detail, structured thinking, and communication are in ensuring that everything works seamlessly in the background.
