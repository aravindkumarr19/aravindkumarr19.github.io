A Day in Asset Operations — What Actually Happens Behind the Scenes

By Aravind · Asset Operations Intern · Chennai

Most people associate fintech with seamless apps, instant transactions, and clean user interfaces.

But what users experience is only the surface.

Behind every transaction is a structured operational layer that ensures money moves correctly, risks are managed, compliance is maintained, and every stakeholder stays aligned.

Working in asset operations has shown me that the real complexity of fintech lies not in what users see, but in everything that happens behind the scenes.

It starts with understanding the deal, not executing it

Before anything is processed, the first priority is clarity.

Every transaction is part of a broader deal structure:

  • Who the stakeholders are
  • How funds are expected to flow
  • What the timelines look like
  • What obligations each party holds

Execution without context is a risk. So the day begins by aligning on the structure.

Financial models define the deal

Financial modeling is not just about numbers—it defines how the deal works.

  • Understanding returns
  • Structuring deals
  • Validating assumptions

Even small inconsistencies can create larger operational issues later.

Documentation makes everything enforceable

Before funds move, documentation must be in place:

  • Discounting Agreements
  • Indemnity Bonds
  • Guarantee Agreements
  • Term Sheets

These define responsibilities, risks, and expectations.

VBA creation and KYC

Virtual Bank Accounts (VBAs) enable structured tracking of funds.

KYC ensures all parties are verified and compliant.

Backend setup

  • Vendor ID creation
  • Asset card creation

This ensures every transaction is tracked properly across systems.

Fund flow coordination

Execution involves aligning with finance teams and ensuring correct allocation of funds.

Precision is critical at this stage.

TDS and compliance

Transactions must also meet regulatory requirements.

  • Correct deductions
  • Compliance tracking

Visibility and communication

Stakeholders require clarity on balances, statements, and transactions.

Providing this builds trust.

Handling real-world challenges

  • Delays in approvals
  • Data mismatches
  • Missing documentation

A key part of the role is navigating these without disruption.

What I’ve learned

Asset operations sits at the intersection of finance, compliance, and execution.

It requires structured thinking, attention to detail, and strong communication.

Closing perspective

Fintech may feel instant to users, but behind it is a carefully coordinated system that makes it work.
